WebD Agency

Timeline

Tools

Figma, Notion, AI

Internal Project

Project Summary

WebD is a fictional web design agency I created to showcase a full-service digital design and development workflow. The objective was to build a conversion-focused website that demonstrates industry-standard practices, emphasizes brand credibility, and highlights the value of strategic, custom-built websites for clients.

Objectives

  • Communicate authority and personality
  • Convert visitors into leads
  • Educate prospects on the process and value of quality design

Goals

  • Establish trust through clean UX
  • Drive conversions with clear CTAs
  • Showcase value proposition effectively
  • Differentiate from generic services

Design Approach

Visual Language

A soft, gradient-infused palette with subtle animations for a modern feel. Rounded UI elements and breathing space promote clarity, while minimal illustrations enable visual storytelling.

Typography

Strong headlines with high contrast to capture attention paired with friendly, clean body copy for maximum readability.

Interaction & Flow

Simple intuitive navigation, sticky lead capture CTAs, and expandable modular sections to keep users engaged without overwhelming them.

Outcome

This project served as both a portfolio centerpiece and a blueprint for future client builds. It illustrates my ability to build conversion-oriented interfaces, my understanding of full-service agency workflows, and my focus on aligning brand storytelling with user experience. The result is a sleek, professional site that reflects the values WebD promises its own clients: clarity, quality, and performance.

Wonderkids

Education & EdTech Platform

Role

UX/UI Designer

Timeline

2 Weeks

Tools

Figma, Illustrator

Type

School Assignment

Project Summary

As part of a school assignment focused on web design and child development, I created an interactive and visually appealing website aimed at making learning enjoyable for children. The goal was to combine education with creativity, ensuring that kids could learn through games, activities, and engaging content.

Objectives

  • Encourage creative learning through interactions
  • Provide fun activities for children
  • Support parents in fostering development

Goals

  • Create an intuitive interface for kids
  • Balance education with gamification
  • Design visually engaging elements
  • Provide resources for parents

Design Approach

Visual Language

Vibrant color palette with purple as the primary brand color. Playful illustrations, animated elements, and rounded soft shapes create a child-friendly appearance.

Typography

Large, readable fonts appropriate for developing readers. Balanced text-to-image ratios and colorful text highlights for key words enhance comprehension.

Content Strategy

Educational content crafted with a fun tone. Simple instructions that children can understand, with specific sections for interactive features (Quiz, Games) and teacher/parent resources.

Outcome

The final website successfully merged learning and play, offering a library of interactive activities, resources for parents, and a visually engaging design that captivated young users. This project deepened my understanding of UX design for children and the importance of creativity in education.
